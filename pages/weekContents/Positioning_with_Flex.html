Positioning with Flex

What is Flex?

Flexbox is a one-dimensional layout method for arranging items in rows or columns. Items flex to fill additional space or shrink to fit into smaller spaces. This article explains all the fundamentals.


Why flexbox?

For a long time, the only reliable cross-browser compatible tools available for creating CSS layouts were features like floats and positioning. These work, but in some ways they're also limiting and frustrating. With the arrival of flexbox, it has become lot of layout tasks much easier.

How do we learn to use flex?

We use the froggy flexbox. It's a game based on the concepts of flex, to learn and exercise its use. He teaches in a playful way through levels, explaining their different uses. Try it on flexboxfroggy.


Where do we use it?

We use for the creation of cards on the home page, the items in the timeline menu and even in the positioning of the contents of the navigation bar. Flexbox is very practical!
